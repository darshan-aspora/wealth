# Aspora Wealth — Design Principles

**Last Updated:** March 8, 2026 | **Status:** Living Document

## 1. Make the App Human

The app should not be cold. It should talk to the user.

### 1a. Give Context or Insight, Not Just Data
Traditional finance products show raw data and expect users to work their way to insight. Every widget and data point exists for a reason. Tell the user **why** they should look at something. Don't present numbers and leave them to figure it out — give them the meaning alongside the metric. Aspora flips this — lead with the human-readable takeaway, and let users drill into the raw numbers if they choose. The surface carries the story. The depth carries the evidence.

### 1b. Bring Personality
The brand voice should be warm, slightly cheeky, and approachable — never corporate, never condescending. The app should feel like a smart friend who knows money, not a Bloomberg terminal. Use animation and imagery sparingly but with intent — the app should have moments that make someone smile.

### 1c. Play for the User
Every CTA, nudge, and piece of copy should **genuinely help** the user — not trick them into engagement. Educate before you sell. Inform before you promote. If you can teach the user something in the moment they're making a decision, do it — even if it slows down the funnel. Never use dark patterns. Never sell on the knowledge platform — directly, indirectly, or in any way ever.

---

## 2. Simple on the Surface, Serious Underneath

The product should feel simple and inviting at first glance, but serious and powerful underneath. The goal is not to dumb things down — it's to be an intelligent company that brings professional tools to retail users in a way that feels natural and easy, not intimidating. A cheetah can smell its prey — pro users will find their loved features. You don't need to blast them on the surface.

### 2a. First Principles on Every Data Point
Don't inherit conventions from other trading apps just because they exist. Question every data point, every label, every unit from first principles — does the user actually need this? Does it help or does it just add noise? Replace professional jargon with plain language. Show fewer things with better context vs. many things without explanation.

### 2b. Breathe Easy
Stock trading already forces red/green colours, charts, complex numbers, and compliance forms onto the screen. Since you can't avoid that, strip everything else down — minimal colours, no unnecessary lines, make it contemporary and minimalistic. The app should feel light because the content is already inherently heavy. It's okay if widgets are longer. The user should see only the essential at first glance, but more data should always be available one level deeper.

### 2c. Speed Is a Feeling
Ultra-low latency. The user should feel the app is fast. Every tap that leads to a spinner erodes confidence, especially when real money is involved. Perceived speed is a trust signal — the app should feel instant and responsive, not like it's waiting on something.

---

## 3. Knowledge Is the Product, Not an Afterthought

In a regulated industry, product features can't be a moat — every broker has access to the same data, APIs, and order types. Knowledge is what's left. It is Aspora's single largest strategic moat, not a nice-to-have.

Educational content and in-app explanations are **strategic pillars**. Every product section should have contextual education woven in. Knowledge softens the app — hard data is for advanced and pro users, storytelling is for the people. People love the storyteller, even if not 100% technically precise, because storytelling builds trust and retention in ways raw data never will.

---

## 4. Stand Out with Small Things

In a regulated sector, you can't go outside the bounding box of regulation. Most apps have the same features. We stand out by doing small things very differently and letting the user notice.

Every element should have **one level of differentiation** from competitors. Every element should be reviewed with one extra layer of thinking and creativity. The question before shipping anything: *"Does our version have that one thing nobody else does?"*

---

## 5. AI Is the Architecture, Not a Feature

AI at Aspora is not a chatbot bolted onto a brokerage app. AI features should feel native to the app experience — integrated into the flow, not pasted on top. The goal is an intelligence layer that enriches every screen and interaction, so the conventional brokerage UI of static watchlists and manual research feels like a relic by comparison.

AI must stay within hard compliance boundaries — no advisory language, always show sources and disclaimers. Consistency between what the UI shows and what the AI says is non-negotiable.
